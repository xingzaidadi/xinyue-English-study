# Initial Cambridge English Diagnostic Table

Version: 2026-06-08

Purpose: decide whether Xinyue should follow the foundation-first route, the A2 Key for Schools route, or the direct B1 Preliminary for Schools route.

This is not an official test. It is a home diagnostic workflow that uses official Cambridge-style materials where possible and records evidence for a family decision.

## 1. Who Can Run This Diagnostic

The executor can be a parent, another family member, an English tutor, or a study assistant.

The executor does not need to be a Cambridge examiner, but should be able to:

- Open the official preparation resources.
- Keep time.
- Record answers and scores.
- Record the child's mood and visible difficulty.
- Avoid giving hints during the diagnostic task.
- Save writing samples and speaking recordings.

If the executor's English is limited, record facts first. Do not force a language-quality judgment.

## 2. What To Prepare

Prepare these before starting:

- A quiet study place.
- Timer.
- Notebook or digital record file.
- Speaking recording device.
- Official A2 Key for Schools sample materials.
- Official B1 Preliminary for Schools sample materials, used only if A2 performance is strong enough.
- Write & Improve or another writing feedback method.

Official links:

- A2 Key for Schools preparation: https://www.cambridgeenglish.org/exams-and-tests/qualifications/key/preparation/
- B1 Preliminary for Schools preparation: https://www.cambridgeenglish.org/exams-and-tests/qualifications/preliminary/preparation/
- Cambridge Test Your English: https://www.cambridgeenglish.org/test-your-english/
- Write & Improve: https://writeandimprove.com/

## 3. Important Rules

- Do not teach during the diagnostic task.
- Do not pause listening audio unless the official task allows it.
- Do not translate every question for the child.
- Do not pressure the child to finish if she becomes strongly upset.
- Mark both scores and emotional load.
- Treat thresholds as planning signals, not official pass predictions.

## 4. Diagnostic Overview

Default schedule:

| Week | Focus | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | A2 Key for Schools diagnostic | Decide whether A2 is too hard, suitable, or easy. |
| Week 2 | A2 follow-up or selected B1 diagnostic | Decide foundation-first, A2-first, or direct B1. |

Recommended total time:

- Weekday session: 30-50 minutes.
- Weekend speaking/writing review: 30-60 minutes.
- Stop early if emotional load is too high.

### Day 5: A2 Writing

Use one short writing task. If using official material, record the exact source. If not using official material, use this prompt:

```text
You want to invite your English friend to your home this Saturday.
Write a short email to your friend.

In your email:
- say why you are inviting your friend
- say what you can do together
- ask one question

Write about 35 words or more.
```

Execution:

1. Give the prompt.
2. Allow 10-15 minutes.
3. Do not correct while the child writes.
4. Save the original writing.
5. Optionally submit to Write & Improve.

### Day 6: A2 Speaking

Set up:

- Record the whole speaking session.
- Keep the session friendly but structured.
- Do not correct during the recording.
- Ask follow-up questions when answers are too short.

Suggested questions:

1. What is your name?
2. How old are you?
3. Where do you live?
4. What subjects do you like at school?
5. What do you usually do after school?
6. Tell me about your best friend.
7. What food do you like? Why?
8. What did you do last weekend?
9. Look at a picture. What can you see?
10. What would you like to do this weekend?

Optional picture task:

- Choose any clear family-friendly picture with people, places, and actions.
- Ask: "What can you see in the picture?"
- Follow up: "What are they doing?" and "Do you like this place? Why?"
