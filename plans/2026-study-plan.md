# 2026 Cambridge English Study Plan

Plan version: 2026-06-08

This plan covers 2026-06-08 through 2026-12-31.

## 1. Assumptions

- Learner: 11 years old, Grade 4.
- Target exams: A2 Key for Schools first by default, then B1 Preliminary for Schools.
- Daily study time: 35-50 minutes on normal weekdays.
- Weekend study time: 45-90 minutes depending on energy and school workload.
- Primary materials: official Cambridge sample tests, vocabulary lists, speaking samples, and writing feedback tools.
- Exam registration: do not assume a fixed month. Confirm available dates, format, and registration deadlines with an authorised exam centre.

## 2. Core Strategy

Do not start with a fixed PET target. Start with diagnosis.

Default route:

```text
2-week diagnosis
  -> A2 Key for Schools 12-week cycle
  -> A2 result/readiness review
  -> B1 Preliminary bridge cycle
```

Direct B1 route is allowed only if the diagnostic result is clearly strong.

## 3. Decision Rules

Use these rules after the initial diagnostic period.

| Diagnostic result | Route |
|---|---|
| A2 reading/listening below about 70%, writing sentences unstable, speaking mostly words or short phrases | Do not register yet. Spend 8-12 weeks on foundations. |
| A2 reading/listening around 70-80%, writing and speaking can complete simple tasks but not fluently | Prepare for A2 Key for Schools first. |
| A2 reading/listening above about 80%, writing task complete, speaking can answer in full sentences | Try selected B1 diagnostic tasks before deciding. |
| B1 reading/listening near 65-70%, writing 80-100 words with clear structure, speaking can sustain simple opinions | Consider direct B1 Preliminary for Schools preparation. |
| Child shows strong resistance, anxiety, or loss of interest | Lower intensity and choose the A2 route or pause exam registration. |

These thresholds are planning signals, not official pass predictions.

## 4. Year Roadmap

### Phase 0: Setup

Dates: 2026-06-08 to 2026-06-09

Goal: prepare materials, records, and expectations.

Actions:

- Download or bookmark official A2 Key for Schools preparation resources.
- Download or bookmark official B1 Preliminary for Schools preparation resources.
- Create a learning record table.
- Choose a fixed daily study window.
- Prepare a phone or computer for speaking recordings.
- Tell the child the first two weeks are only for finding the right level, not for ranking or judging.

Output:

- Official resource links collected.
- Study record table ready.
- Initial diagnostic schedule ready.

### Phase 1: Initial Diagnosis

Dates: 2026-06-10 to 2026-06-23

Goal: decide A2-first, B1-direct, or foundation-first.

Week 1 focuses on A2:

| Day | Task | Parent focus |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | A2 Reading & Writing sample tasks | Record accuracy, time, and obvious pain points. |
| Tuesday | Review reading/writing errors | Classify errors: vocabulary, grammar, task misunderstanding, locating information. |
| Wednesday | A2 Listening sample tasks | Record accuracy and what was missed. |
| Thursday | Listening review | Replay wrong items and note whether the issue is speed, vocabulary, numbers, names, or attention. |
| Friday | A2 short writing task | Save the writing text and feedback. |
| Saturday | A2-style speaking simulation, 8-10 minutes | Record audio and note fluency, sentence completeness, and willingness to speak. |
| Sunday | Light English input only | No test pressure. |

Week 2 depends on Week 1:

| Week 1 result | Week 2 action |
|---|---|
| A2 is difficult | Continue A2 skill checks and foundation review. Do not attempt full B1. |
| A2 is mostly manageable | Try selected B1 reading/listening tasks as an exposure check. |
| A2 is easy | Complete a small B1 diagnostic set across reading, listening, writing, and speaking. |

Output:

- Diagnostic summary.
- Route decision on 2026-06-24.

### Phase 2A: A2 Foundation and Exam Skills

Dates: 2026-06-24 to 2026-07-21

Use this phase if the route is A2-first.

Goal: stabilize A2 vocabulary, task reading, listening habits, and basic sentence output.

Weekly structure:

| Skill | Frequency | Time | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Vocabulary in context | 4 times/week | 10-15 minutes | Use sample tasks and short sentences. Avoid isolated word lists only. |
| Reading | 3 times/week | 15-25 minutes | Start untimed, then add light timing. |
| Listening | 4 times/week | 10-15 minutes | Replay wrong answers and listen for key words. |
| Writing | 2 times/week | 15-25 minutes | Short emails, notes, and simple story sentences. |
| Speaking | 2 times/week | 8-12 minutes | Record, replay, and correct only 1-2 priorities. |

Milestone on 2026-07-21:

- Reading and listening should feel less stressful.
- Child should complete A2-style writing without freezing.
- Speaking should move from words/phrases to short complete sentences.

### Phase 3A: A2 Exam Preparation

Dates: 2026-07-22 to 2026-08-18

Goal: connect ability to exam format.

Actions:

- Practice each A2 part separately.
- Build a small list of recurring personal errors.
- Start limited timed practice.
- Continue speaking twice per week.
- Continue writing twice per week.
- Do one half mock or carefully selected full A2 practice near the end of the phase.

Milestone on 2026-08-18:

- Reading/listening should be around 75% or higher on familiar-format practice.
- Writing should finish the required task and include all information points.
- Speaking should be understandable, even if not perfect.

### Phase 4A: A2 Readiness and Registration Check

Dates: 2026-08-19 to 2026-09-15

Goal: decide whether to register for the nearest suitable A2 Key for Schools exam.

Actions:

- Complete 1-2 full A2 simulations under light exam conditions.
- Review whether wrong answers are decreasing.
- Contact authorised exam centres for exam dates, format, location, fees, and deadlines.
- If readiness is weak, delay registration and continue training.
- If readiness is strong, register for the nearest suitable A2 Key for Schools session.

Readiness signs:

- Child can finish the tasks without major anxiety.
- Reading/listening results are stable enough across more than one practice.
- Writing is complete and understandable.
- Speaking is cooperative and uses full sentences.

### Phase 5A: A2 Exam Window or Maintenance

Dates: 2026-09-16 to 2026-10-06

Goal: either take A2 or maintain readiness until the actual exam date.

If exam is scheduled:

- Reduce workload in the final week.
- Keep speaking short and frequent.
- Do not introduce many new materials.
- Review practical exam-day flow.

If exam is not scheduled:

- Continue one A2 maintenance task per week.
- Start light B1 bridge content.

### Phase 6A: B1 Bridge After A2

Dates: 2026-10-07 to 2026-11-17

Goal: move from A2 task completion to B1-level thinking and expression.

Focus areas:

- Longer reading passages.
- Guessing meaning from context.
- Common B1 topic vocabulary: school, hobbies, travel, environment, technology, health, opinions.
- Writing 80-100 word emails and simple stories.
- Speaking with reasons: because, so, but, however, for example.

Weekly structure:

| Skill | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Reading | 3 times/week | Mix Cambridge-style tasks and graded reading. |
| Listening | 3-4 times/week | Use short clips and official-style audio. |
| Writing | 2 times/week | Require planning before writing. |
| Speaking | 2 times/week | Practice opinions and picture description. |
| Vocabulary | 4 times/week | Learn phrases, not only single words. |

### Phase 7A: B1 Readiness Review

Dates: 2026-11-18 to 2026-12-31

Goal: decide whether 2027 B1 exam preparation should start formally.

Actions:

- Try selected B1 Preliminary for Schools tasks.
- Compare performance with the June diagnostic baseline.
- Build a 2027 B1 plan.
- Decide whether the next target is:
  - B1 exam in the first half of 2027.
  - More B1 foundation first.
  - A slower mixed route if school workload increases.

End-of-year target:

- Child has a clear A2 foundation.
- Family knows whether B1 is a near-term exam goal or a longer-term ability goal.
- Study rhythm is sustainable.

## 5. Alternative Route: Direct B1

Use this only if the diagnostic result is strong.

### Phase 2B: B1 Bridge

Dates: 2026-06-24 to 2026-08-18

Goal: close the gap between strong A2 and real B1.

Focus:

- B1 vocabulary by topic.
- Longer reading.
- Listening for detail and inference.
- Writing 80-100 words.
- Speaking opinions and reasons.

### Phase 3B: B1 Task Training

Dates: 2026-08-19 to 2026-10-27

Goal: become familiar with all B1 Preliminary for Schools parts.

Actions:

- Train reading parts separately.
- Train writing email and story/article structure.
- Practice listening with note-taking.
- Practice speaking: personal questions, picture description, discussion.

### Phase 4B: B1 Mock and Decision

Dates: 2026-10-28 to 2026-12-08

Goal: decide whether a late-2026 or early-2027 B1 exam is realistic.

Actions:

- Complete 2-3 B1 simulations.
- Review readiness and emotional load.
- Contact exam centres only if results are stable.

### Phase 5B: Consolidation

Dates: 2026-12-09 to 2026-12-31

Goal: consolidate B1 and prepare a 2027 plan.

## 6. Weekly Template

Use this as the default weekly schedule.

| Day | Main task | Secondary task |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Reading | Vocabulary in context |
| Tuesday | Listening | Error review |
| Wednesday | Writing | Sentence upgrade |
| Thursday | Reading | Speaking |
| Friday | Listening | Vocabulary in context |
| Saturday | Practice set or half mock | Speaking or writing review |
| Sunday | Light input | No formal testing |

## 7. Daily Template

Normal day:

| Time | Activity |
|---:|---|
| 5 minutes | Review yesterday's errors. |
| 15-20 minutes | Main reading or listening task. |
| 10 minutes | Review mistakes and write the reason. |
| 8-12 minutes | Short writing or speaking recording. |
| 3-5 minutes | Parent records mood and next action. |

## 8. Error Categories

Use these categories in the record table.

| Category | Meaning |
|---|---|
| vocabulary | Did not know the word or phrase. |
| grammar | Grammar blocked understanding or output. |
| task | Misread what the question asked. |
| locating | Could not find the answer in the text. |
| inference | Needed to infer meaning or attitude. |
| listening-sound | Missed pronunciation, linking, names, numbers, or speed. |
| writing-content | Did not include required information. |
| writing-structure | Ideas were not organized. |
| speaking-fluency | Too many pauses or only short answers. |
| speaking-expansion | Could not add reasons or examples. |
| emotion | Stress, boredom, resistance, or fatigue affected performance. |

## 9. Parent Rules

- Praise effort and strategy, not only scores.
- Correct only the most important 1-2 problems after each speaking recording.
- Do not use full mock tests too early.
- If the child is frustrated for two consecutive days, reduce difficulty for the next session.
- Keep one day per week free from formal testing.
- Treat exam registration as a readiness decision, not a fixed promise.

## 10. Tooling Roadmap

The first tool should be a lightweight study-coach skill, not a full app.

Planned sequence:

1. Create diagnostic table.
2. Create `ket-pet-study-coach` skill.
3. Use the skill for weekly planning and review.
4. After 4-6 weeks, evaluate whether an app is worth building.

Possible future app features:

- Daily task view.
- Timer.
- Speaking recorder.
- Writing submission area.
- Error tagging.
- Parent weekly report.
- A2/B1 readiness indicator.
