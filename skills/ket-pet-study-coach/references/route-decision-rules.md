# Route Decision Rules

Use these rules after the two-week diagnostic.

## Foundation-First

Choose this if:

- A2 reading/listening is below about 70%.
- Writing is incomplete or hard to understand.
- Speaking is blocked or mostly words.
- Emotional load is high.

Focus on basic A2 skills for 8-12 weeks before exam preparation.

## A2 Key For Schools First

Choose this if:

- A2 is manageable but not easy.
- B1 is not tested or clearly too hard.
- Writing and speaking can complete simple tasks but are not stable.
- The family wants a safer confidence-building route.

This is the default route for most primary-school learners unless evidence supports direct B1.

## Direct B1 Preliminary For Schools

Consider this only if:

- A2 is easy.
- B1 reading/listening is near 65-70% or better.
- Writing can reach about 80-100 words with clear structure.
- Speaking can give reasons and continue for 30-60 seconds.
- The child remains calm and willing.

## Delay Exam Decision

Choose this if:

- Scores are mixed.
- School workload is high.
- The child is anxious or resistant.
- The family lacks enough diagnostic evidence.
